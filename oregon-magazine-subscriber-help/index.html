FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I am entitled to overtime pay?

California employees are entitled to overtime pay if they work more than 40-hours a week or more than eight hours in a workday. There are only a few exceptions to this rule.

Can I qualify for overtime pay if I am paid a day rate, lump sum amount per job, or salary?

Yes. The way you are paid (day rate, lump sum, or salary) does not affect whether you are entitled to overtime pay or other benefits.

What about meal and rest breaks?

California employees are entitled to a daily lunch break and regular rest breaks. There are only a few exceptions to this rule.

What if I did not receive overtime pay or regular meal and rest breaks?

You may be entitled to compensation, including past-due overtime pay, premium pay for missed meal and rest breaks, and penalty payments.
