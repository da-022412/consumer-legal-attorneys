Why would I be entitled to compensation?

New York Labor Law requires employers to pay their employees once per week if they spend more than 25% of their working time performing manual labor (i.e., stocking shelves, tending cash registers, assisting customers). If you have been paid less frequently (i.e., once every two weeks), you may be able to sue to recover substantial money due to being paid late.
